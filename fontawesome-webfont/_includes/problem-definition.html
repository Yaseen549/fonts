It's time for a better Web

Browsers are built for a read-only Web

The Web was imagined as a space for creation â€” the first Web browser even had built-in tools for editing webpages! But browsers of today are built for consumption, not creation, hiding away View Source and casting aside the vision of a read-write Web.

Beaker makes creating a first-class citizen of the browser, with tools for exploring and remixing a website's files, one-click publishing, and truly serverless hosting. Whether you're an HTML hobbyist or a seasoned developer, Beaker is built to help you build.

Apps are locked away in the cloud

We depend on apps for the professional to the deeply personal, yet we're powerless to shape how they work. Feature requests for chronological feeds and moderation tools fall second to what drives engagement and ad revenue, even when it's not best for users.

Beaker is a browser for a customizable Web, where communities can moderate themselves, feed algorithms are transparent and tweakable, and every user is empowered to define their online experiences.

Centralization stifles creativity

Platforms like Facebook, Tencent, and Twitter enabled mass communication and connection, but their efforts to stop cross-platform interoperability keep users locked in, constraining Web publishing to the narrow bounds of their toolsets.

Beaker enables an interoperable Web, where developers can build apps on top of user-owned data. With no central platform pulling the strings, users are free to move their data between apps, exploring new ways of publishing, creating, and connecting on the Web.
